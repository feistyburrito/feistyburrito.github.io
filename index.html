Let's talk about mobility.

I used to be active and engaged in lot's of acitivities both outdors and indoors. Lately, I'm more prone to injuries and it takes longer to heal from them. In reflection, I can say there's a big difference to how freely my physical movements were compared to the present. So let's talk about mobility. Forget about the whole aethetics of a fit body, let's address mobility as we age. Ageing affects muscle mass and strength, bone density, and can make joints stiffer and less flexible due to reduction of cartilage thickness. As we age, muscles, bones and joints undergo physiological changes that affect mobility, which can ultimately impact our independence. Age-related changes to muscles, bones and joints can be reversed. I can start by living a more active lifestyle. Not only will I feel better with everyday movements, but I will also be able to get back to the life and activities I enjoy. This has to be done by stages to make sure the habit stays. Assesing my current activity level and increasing it even by 1% daily will help me in the long run. Mobility is important but a lot of young folks like myself take it for granted.


5 tips for maintaining lifelong mobility.

  • Warm-Up
  • Practice Stretching and Foam Rolling
  • Go for a Daily Walk
  • Improve Balance and Coordination
  • Try Low Impact Cardio
